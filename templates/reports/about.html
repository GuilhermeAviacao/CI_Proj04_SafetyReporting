Aviation Safety Reporting Initiave

Building a safer aviation community through voluntary reporting

Why Safety Reporting Matters

Aviation is safe greatly due to voluntary safety reporting! It is one of the most powerful tools for accident avoidance. Every report submitted contributes to a collective understanding of risks and helps prevent future incidents.

Safety Reporting Principles

Accountable

Secure login required. All reports are traceable to promote responsible reporting and data integrity.

Non-Punitive

Focus on system improvement rather than blaming and individual fault-finding.

Action-Oriented

Reports trigger real investigations, driving improvements and preventive measures.

Benefits of Reporting

  • Proactive Safety:

    Identify and address potential hazards before they become accidents

  • Data-Driven Insights:

    Collect valuable data to understand trends and patterns in aviation operations

  • Learning Culture:

    Foster an environment where everyone learns from shared experiences

  • Continuous Improvement:

    Drive ongoing enhancements to procedures, training, and systems

What to Report

  • Near Misses:

    Situations that could have resulted in an accident

  • Equipment Issues:

    Malfunctions, failures, or unexpected equipment behavior

  • Procedural Concerns:

    Unclear procedures or process improvement opportunities

  • Environmental Factors:

    Weather, wildlife, or facility-related safety observations

The Safety Pyramid: Every Fatality is Preceded by Warning Signs

Aviation safety research shows a clear pattern: before one fatality occurs, there are typically 10 serious incidents, 30 minor incidents, 600 near misses, and 300,000 unsafe acts.
